SAN DIEGO \u2014 United States Women\u2019s National Team\u00a0forward Alex Morgan \u2014 a hometown favorite on the San Diego Wave FC team \u2014 is banking rolling as the highest-paid player at 2023 FIFA Women\u2019s World Cup.<\/p>\n

That\u2019s according to Forbes<\/a>, a finance and business magazine that crunched the numbers to see just how much each player is making to determine the top earners of the world tournament.<\/p>\n

Forbes considered both on-field earnings and off-field earnings, which includes each players \u00a0endorsement portfolios and side-business ventures. <\/p>\n

San Diego\u2019s Alex Morgan nets $0.8 million for her dedication to the field, while also bringing in another $6.3 million, according to Forbes. That puts her at $7.1 million in earnings. <\/p>\n

Morgan\u2019s swelling bank account can be attributed to her endorsement deals with Hublot, Cola-Cola\u2019s Bodyarmor and Anheuser-Busch\u2019s Michelob Ultra. <\/p>\n

The striker is also a cofounder of media company Togethxr<\/a>, which promotes storytelling highlighting equity, diversity and inclusion. In fact, Forbes says Morgan is producing a Netflix docuseries championed by Togethxr that will give an inside look at this year\u2019s World Cup run.<\/p>\n

\n<\/aside>\n

The second highest-paid player at 2023 FIFA Women\u2019s World Cup, according to Forbes, is Team USA\u2019s Megan Rapinoe, who plays regular season for OL Reign.<\/p>\n

Rapinoe nets $0.7 million for her efforts on the field and another $6.3 million in off-field earnings, which includes her equity stake in a plant-based food startup known as Happy Viking, Forbes noted. This business was launched by tennis legend Venus Williams.<\/p>\n

In collaboration with her partner \u2014 WNBA star Sue Bird \u2014 Rapinoe also launched a production company called A Touch More<\/a>.\u00a0The 38-year-old player also had the most branded social media posts<\/a> of any women\u2019s soccer player. <\/p>\n

In total, 11 USWNT\u00a0players landed on Forbes\u2019 list of 15 highest-paid players kicking turf at this year\u2019s Women\u2019s World Cup. The full list can be found here<\/a>.<\/p>\n

\n<\/aside>\n

All money aside, Team USA has won two straight Women\u2019s World Cup titles and are now vying for their third on the world stage. <\/p>\n
