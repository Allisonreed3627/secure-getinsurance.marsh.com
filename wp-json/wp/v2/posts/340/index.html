On any given summer day, Huntington Beach is one of the busiest beaches in the country \u2013 but add the US Open of Surfing, and a day at the beach can turn into a massive maze of festivities to navigate.<\/p>\n

Don\u2019t worry, we\u2019re here to help if you want to venture into what\u2019s considered the world\u2019s largest action-sports festival, a nine-day event that is expected to draw hundreds of thousands of people to the south side of the Huntington Beach Pier between Saturday, July 29, and Aug. 6<\/a>.<\/p>\n

The best tip to avoid frustration: Get there early.<\/p>\n

Parking will be gobbled up quickly with surf action kicking off early in the morning, so if you want a prime spot on the sand or pier to watch, it\u2019s best to beat the crowds.<\/p>\n

Here\u2019s some tips about the schedule, parking, where to eat and happenings off the sand and around town to know before you go to the Wallex U.S. Open of Surfing Presented by Pacifico:<\/p>\n

Schedule<\/h4>\n

The surf action kicks off first thing Saturday morning, with most days starting at 7:30 a.m. and running until about 3:30 p.m., though bogus weather such as foggy conditions that cloud the judges\u2019 view of the surfers or funky winds could cause delays.<\/p>\n

The finals will wrap up on Aug. 6 at about 2:15 p.m. The awards ceremony happens from 2:30 to 3 p.m. on the sand just south of the pier.<\/p>\n

The US Open retail store and welcome center opens at 8 a.m. each day, and food trucks on the sand will be dishing up as early as 8:30 a.m. and be open until 4 p.m.<\/p>\n

Sponsor and partner activations kick off at 9 a.m. each day. With about 40 sponsors signed on this year<\/a>, there should be plenty of free stuff to nab on the sand or games to play at the various booths. Typically, more brands participate later in the week and the final weekend.<\/p>\n

The biggest buzz this year is the addition of Nitro Circus\u2019 \u201cFull Throttle FMX,\u201d with pro freestyle motocross action in a massive 305-foot ramp-to-ramp demo.<\/p>\n

The half-hour shows will happen July 29 and 30 and Aug. 5 at three different time slots: 11 a.m., 1:30 p.m. and 3:30 p.m.<\/p>\n

There will be a few athlete signings down on the sand. Hawaiian pro surfer Coco Ho will be signing autographs on July 29 from noon to 1 p.m. and food company Sambazon<\/a> will have an athlete signing at the same time the following day with San Clemente\u2019s Sawyer Lindblad, Ella McCaffrey and Sara Freyere, a Huntington Beach stand-out who just earned the NSSA National Championship.<\/a><\/p>\n

Mid-week, the action is mostly focused on the surf and beach festival. On Aug. 2, a \u201cCup Noodles Geo Selfie Live\u201d is planned, though it\u2019s unclear from the schedule what that\u2019s all about \u2014 but hopefully there\u2019s some fun noodle slurping involved.<\/p>\n

On Aug. 3, there is a WSL Ocean x Shiseido Public Beach Cleanup planned to help pick up any mess left on the beach.<\/p>\n

That same day, the first scheduled music performance by Sweet Nobody will happen on the main stage at 2 p.m. On Aug. 4, a music performance by Nice & Swell is planned. On Aug. 5, Wes Chiller will take the stage for the final music performance.<\/p>\n

The final day is usually pretty mellow in the festival area with the spotlight on the surfers out in the water battling for the prestigious US Open of Surfing title.<\/p>\n
