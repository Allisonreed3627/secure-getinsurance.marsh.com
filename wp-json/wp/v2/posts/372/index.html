SAN DIEGO \u2014 Can you guess which San Diego metro area city has the most affordable housing market?<\/p>\n

If you don\u2019t want to spend over half of your yearly income towards homeownership, then San Diego may not be the place for you \u2014 hypothetically speaking. <\/p>\n

Based on the average projected income of a household, median asking price, estimated mortgage cost and taxes, there\u2019s only one San Diego metro area city where homebuyers will be forking out less than 50% of their income for a place to call their own.<\/p>\n

That\u2019s according to a report<\/a> by real estate listing platform RealtyHop, which examined home affordability for the average American family in the 100 largest U.S. cities and their surrounding metros.<\/p>\n

<\/aside>\n

Based on this report\u2019s key findings, Carlsbad is the least affordable housing market in the regional metro area. RealtyHop says households in Carlsbad spend 69.03% of their income on mortgage and property tax payments.<\/p>\n

Next in line as least affordable comes Escondido, where homeowners spend 65.64% of their income on housing costs annually, this report shows.<\/p>\n

\n<\/aside>\n

Then comes San Diego as the third least affordable city in the region, with homeowners spending 57.31% of their income and Oceanside in fourth place with residents dishing out 55.78% of their yearly earnings, according to RealtyHop.<\/p>\n

The most affordable San Diego metro area city is Chula Vista, where homeowners spend 48.26% of their earned money for the year. This South Bay community also has the lowest median purchase price for a home at $725,000, the report shows. <\/p>\n
