SAN DIEGO \u2014 A freeway crash near the U.S.-Mexico border left at least one person dead Wednesday afternoon, the California Highway Patrol said.<\/p>\n

The crash happened shortly before noon on southbound Interstate 805 near Camino De La Plaza, which is the last U.S. exit.<\/p>\n

According to CHP\u2019s Traffic Incident Information Page, a witness reported seeing a white sedan heading towards the port of entry make a U-turn on the freeway then hit a Honda minivan.<\/p>\n

CHP confirmed that the driver of the white vehicle, an Audi, was driving the wrong-way at the time of the crash, hitting the van head on.<\/p>\n

\u201cOur understanding at this point is they turned around at the point of entry and came the wrong way,\u201d\u00a0CHP Sgt. Hope Maxson said.<\/p>\n

It appeared the driver may have missed the last U.S. exit, and began driving the wrong way on I-805.<\/p>\n

A Sig Alert was issued by Caltrans for the right two lanes on southbound I-805 north of Camino De La Plaza. The closure was expected to last several hours, according to CHP.<\/p>\n

A man driving the minivan was killed as a result of the crash. <\/p>\n

The driver of the Audi, a 29-year-old man, was transported to a nearby hospital with major, but not life-threatening injuries, CHP said.<\/p>\n
